\section{Limitations and future work}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Workflow structure vs.\ LLM semantics.}
Agentproof verifies properties of the workflow \emph{topology} and the event
stream emitted by execution. It does not prove semantic properties of LLM
outputs (e.g., factuality or intent), which depend on non-deterministic model
behavior and prompt/context choices.

\paragraph{Extractor best-effort.}
Frameworks evolve quickly and expose different internal representations across
versions. The extractors in Agentproof are designed to be robust, but they
inevitably involve heuristics (e.g., recognizing human review nodes by naming or
metadata).

\paragraph{Temporal DSL expressiveness.}
The current temporal policy DSL is intentionally small and focused on common
safety patterns; it is not a full LTL implementation. Extending to richer
temporal fragments and to full graph $\times$ automaton model checking is a
natural next step.

\paragraph{Library integration.}
Some structural analyses are currently demonstrated in examples. Future work
includes packaging a stable verification API that returns structured reports and
counterexample traces, suitable for CI gating and developer feedback.
