\section{Temporal DSL grammar}
\label{app:grammar}

The full BNF grammar for the temporal policy DSL:

\begin{verbatim}
<rule>       ::= <forbidden>
               | <impl_future>
               | <until>
               | <bounded>
               | <chain>
               | <conjunction>
               | <disjunction>

<forbidden>  ::= "G" "!" <atom>

<impl_future>::= <atom> "->" "F" <atom>

<until>      ::= <atom> "U" <atom>

<bounded>    ::= <atom> "->" "F[<=" <int> "]" <atom>

<chain>      ::= <atom> ("->" "F" <atom>){2,}

<conjunction>::= "(" <rule> ")" "AND" "(" <rule> ")"

<disjunction>::= "(" <rule> ")" "OR" "(" <rule> ")"

<atom>       ::= "tool:" <name>
               | "action:" <name>
               | "decision:" <name>
               | <tag_name>

<name>       ::= [a-zA-Z_][a-zA-Z0-9_]*
<tag_name>   ::= [a-zA-Z_][a-zA-Z0-9_]*
<int>        ::= [1-9][0-9]*
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{Predicate matching.}
Atomic predicates are matched against event dictionaries:
\texttt{tool:X} matches when the event's \texttt{tool\_name} field
equals \texttt{X}; \texttt{action:X} matches \texttt{action\_type};
\texttt{decision:X} matches the \texttt{decision} field; bare names
match membership in the event's \texttt{tags} set.
